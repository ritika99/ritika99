Be it designing distributed, low-latency communication systems that power global enterprises or building event-driven architectures that automate observability and resilience, I’m passionate about transforming complex problems into scalable, measurable solutions.

As a Software Development Engineer II at Amazon Web Services (AWS) Connect in Seattle, I work on backend systems that deliver reliable, real-time customer experiences at scale. My journey has been shaped by a constant drive to merge strong engineering fundamentals with innovation, from optimizing APIs and cost efficiency to integrating GenAI-powered tools that enhance developer productivity and customer satisfaction.

Previously, I earned my Master’s in Artificial Intelligence and Innovation from Carnegie Mellon University, where I explored the intersection of machine learning, systems, and data engineering. I’ve applied ML to diverse domains from speech emotion recognition using modified neural networks to recommendation systems and LLM-based log analytics pipelines at AWS.

Beyond engineering, I actively mentor and review for conferences like Grace Hopper Celebration and journals such as Springer Nature’s Multimedia Tools and Applications, reflecting my passion for research and community engagement.
